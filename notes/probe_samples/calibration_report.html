Do 156-step probes predict 780-step results?

2026-07-09 · calibration check 4 directions · 2 lengths · DINOv2 centroid similarity pod cn0zudkxb89or6 · RTX 4090
Verdict
No — rankings disagree between probe and full length. Probes can screen out a catastrophically bad direction, but cannot be trusted to pick a winner.

The reversal

Four hyperparameter directions were each trained to a 156-step probe length and a full 780-step length, then scored by cosine similarity to the real-art style centroid in DINOv2 embedding space. A direction that ranks the same way at both lengths means probes can be trusted; one that doesn't means only the full run can be.

156 steps (probe)780 steps (full run)

constlr goes from best at 156 steps to worst at 780 — a reversal of roughly 0.10, the largest move on the chart. Its constant learning rate never decays the way the other three directions' cosine schedule does, so it keeps training at full strength the whole way, which looks strong early and costs it quality by the end. dim64 is the one point of full agreement: worst at both lengths, by a wide enough margin (0.06+) to trust regardless of noise. control and lr2e4 swap the top spot too, but by only about 0.03 — small enough that it might just be noise, not yet confirmed either way.

What this changes for round 1

The methodology's own decision rule (lab notebook, Section 3, step 1) already covered this case: when probe-length and full-length rankings disagree, probes are downgraded from "pick the winner" to "rule out the disasters." That's still useful — dim64 would have been correctly screened out from a 156-step probe alone — but it means every direction that survives round 1's probe phase now needs its own full 780-step commit run before being trusted, which raises the real GPU-hour cost of the round versus the original plan.

  1. Measure the real noise floor
    Pairwise score deltas between pooled control-only replicates, same fixed prompt/seed slots. Needed before the control/lr2e4 swap can be called noise or real.
  2. Derive round 1's replicate count
    Simulate the permutation test at a few candidate n using the measured noise floor plus an injected 0.02-cosine effect; keep the smallest n with ≥80% detection.
  3. Run round 1's probe phase
    ~10 candidate directions at the derived n, screening only — not ranking — against pooled controls.
  4. Apply the selection rule
    Permutation/bootstrap test, 0.02-cosine effect floor, Benjamini–Hochberg correction across all directions tested.
  5. Commit and score
    Full 780-step retrain of the surviving direction, scored at epochs 2/4/6/8/final against original and holdout prompt sets.
